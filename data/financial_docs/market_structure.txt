US MARKET STRUCTURE — REFERENCE DOCUMENT

MAJOR US EXCHANGES
- NYSE (New York Stock Exchange): Oldest and largest by market cap (~$25T). Blue-chip stocks. Uses designated market makers (DMMs) for liquidity provision.
- NASDAQ: Technology-heavy. Fully electronic. Home to Apple, Microsoft, Alphabet, Nvidia, Meta, Amazon.
- CBOE: Chicago-based. Now primarily an options exchange. CBOE Volatility Index (VIX) is calculated from S&P 500 options.
- NYSE Arca: ETF listing venue; largest ETF exchange.

MAJOR INDICES
- S&P 500: 500 large-cap US companies by float-adjusted market cap. The benchmark for institutional performance. Rebalanced quarterly.
- NASDAQ-100 (QQQ): Top 100 non-financial NASDAQ companies. Heavily weighted to mega-cap tech.
- Dow Jones Industrial Average (DJIA): 30 price-weighted blue-chip stocks. Less representative than the S&P 500.
- Russell 2000 (IWM): 2000 small-cap US stocks. Leading indicator for domestic economic conditions.
- VIX (VIXY): "Fear index." Derived from 30-day implied volatility of S&P 500 options. Mean-reverts around 20.

SECTORS (GICS CLASSIFICATION)
The Global Industry Classification Standard divides the market into 11 sectors:
1. Information Technology (XLK) — ~29% of S&P 500; Apple, Microsoft, Nvidia
2. Healthcare (XLV) — ~13%; UnitedHealth, Eli Lilly, Johnson & Johnson
3. Financials (XLF) — ~13%; JPMorgan, Berkshire, Visa
4. Consumer Discretionary (XLY) — ~10%; Amazon, Tesla, Home Depot
5. Communication Services (XLC) — ~9%; Alphabet, Meta, Netflix
6. Industrials (XLI) — ~8%; Caterpillar, Union Pacific, Honeywell
7. Consumer Staples (XLP) — ~6%; Procter & Gamble, Coca-Cola, Walmart
8. Energy (XLE) — ~4%; ExxonMobil, Chevron, ConocoPhillips
9. Real Estate (XLRE) — ~2%; Prologis, American Tower, Equinox
10. Materials (XLB) — ~2%; Linde, Sherwin-Williams, Freeport-McMoRan
11. Utilities (XLU) — ~2%; NextEra Energy, Duke Energy, Southern Company

MARKET CAPITALIZATION CATEGORIES
- Mega-cap: > $200B (Apple, Microsoft, Nvidia, Alphabet, Amazon, Meta)
- Large-cap: $10B–$200B (most S&P 500 companies)
- Mid-cap: $2B–$10B (many Russell 1000 non-S&P 500 companies)
- Small-cap: $300M–$2B (Russell 2000 territory)
- Micro-cap: < $300M (higher risk, lower liquidity)

TRADING SESSIONS
- Pre-market: 4:00 AM – 9:30 AM ET (lower liquidity, wider spreads)
- Regular session: 9:30 AM – 4:00 PM ET (primary trading hours)
- After-hours: 4:00 PM – 8:00 PM ET (companies often report earnings after close)

ORDER TYPES
- Market Order: Execute immediately at best available price. Risk: slippage in volatile markets.
- Limit Order: Execute only at specified price or better. Risk: may not fill.
- Stop-Loss Order: Trigger a market sell when price falls to a set level. Risk: gap downs can cause worse-than-expected execution.
- Stop-Limit Order: Trigger a limit order at the stop price. Risk: may not fill in fast markets.

LIQUIDITY AND SPREADS
The bid-ask spread is the cost of immediately buying vs. selling. Liquid stocks (Apple, SPY) have spreads of 1 cent. Illiquid small-caps may have spreads of 1–2%. Average daily volume (ADV) measures liquidity. Trading more than 1% of ADV typically moves the market price.

ETFs VS. STOCKS
ETFs (Exchange-Traded Funds) track indices, sectors, commodities, or strategies. They trade like stocks throughout the day. The creation/redemption mechanism keeps ETF prices close to their NAV. ETFs offer diversification, low costs, and tax efficiency. The largest ETF is SPY ($500B+ AUM, expense ratio 0.09%).

SHORT SELLING
Short sellers borrow shares, sell them, and hope to buy back at a lower price. Short interest (% of float sold short) signals bearish sentiment. High short interest (> 20%) can trigger a short squeeze if the stock rises (shorts forced to buy to cover). The GameStop (GME) episode in 2021 was a famous retail-driven short squeeze.

MARKET MICROSTRUCTURE — IMPORTANT FOR AGENTS
When evaluating stock data from APIs like Alpha Vantage:
- "Global Quote" provides real-time price, volume, daily high/low
- "OVERVIEW" provides fundamental data (P/E, market cap, 52-week range)
- Data is 15-minutes delayed on free tiers; real-time on premium
- Market cap = Current Price × Shares Outstanding (not float)
- Float = Shares Outstanding - Insider Shares - Restricted Shares
