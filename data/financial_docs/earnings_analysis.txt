EARNINGS ANALYSIS FRAMEWORK — REFERENCE DOCUMENT

WHAT IS AN EARNINGS REPORT?
Publicly traded companies report financial results quarterly (10-Q) and annually (10-K) to the SEC. These include the income statement, balance sheet, and cash flow statement. Earnings season refers to the ~3-week period after each quarter ends when most S&P 500 companies report.

THE INCOME STATEMENT
Key lines to analyze:
- Revenue (Top Line): Total sales. Look for YoY growth, sequential growth, and guidance revision.
- Gross Profit = Revenue - Cost of Goods Sold. Gross margin = Gross Profit / Revenue.
- Operating Income (EBIT): After SG&A and R&D. Operating margin shows operational efficiency.
- EBITDA: Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, and Amortization. Strips out capital structure effects.
- Net Income (Bottom Line): After taxes and interest. Can be distorted by one-time charges.
- EPS (Earnings Per Share): Net Income divided by diluted share count. Most watched number.

EARNINGS BEATS AND MISSES
Wall Street analysts publish consensus EPS and revenue estimates before results. A "beat" means actuals exceeded consensus; a "miss" means they fell short. Historically, the S&P 500 beats by ~5% EPS on average (analysts tend to be conservative). The market reaction often depends on guidance more than the actual results — stocks can fall on a beat if guidance disappoints.

KEY METRICS BEYOND EPS
- Free Cash Flow (FCF): Operating Cash Flow - Capital Expenditures. More reliable than reported EPS (harder to manipulate). FCF margin = FCF / Revenue.
- Same-Store Sales (SSS): Retail metric comparing comparable locations. Strips out growth from new store openings.
- Monthly Active Users (MAU), Daily Active Users (DAU): Critical for platform/tech companies.
- Net Revenue Retention (NRR) / Dollar Retention: SaaS metric. NRR > 120% means existing customers expand faster than churn.
- Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR) and ARR growth: SaaS primary growth metric.
- Bookings, Billings, Backlog: Forward-looking revenue indicators.
- Gross Merchandise Volume (GMV): For marketplace companies; total transaction value.

BALANCE SHEET ANALYSIS
- Cash and Equivalents: Runway for cash-burning companies. At current burn rate, how many quarters can the company operate?
- Debt-to-Equity (D/E): Financial leverage. High D/E increases bankruptcy risk but can magnify returns.
- Interest Coverage Ratio = EBIT / Interest Expense. Must be > 1 to cover debt payments. < 2x is concerning.
- Working Capital = Current Assets - Current Liabilities. Positive working capital means short-term liquidity.
- Book Value per Share: Net assets per share. Compare to market price for P/B ratio.

CASH FLOW STATEMENT
Three sections:
1. Operating Cash Flow: Cash generated from core business operations. Should be positive and growing.
2. Investing Cash Flow: Capital expenditures (CAPEX), acquisitions, asset sales. Usually negative (investment).
3. Financing Cash Flow: Debt issuance/repayment, share buybacks, dividends.

RED FLAGS IN EARNINGS
- Revenue growing faster than cash flow (potential revenue recognition issues)
- Increasing accounts receivable relative to revenue (customers not paying)
- Rising inventory relative to revenue (demand slowdown)
- Goodwill impairments (overpaid acquisitions)
- Frequent "one-time" charges that recur every quarter
- Stock-based compensation (SBC) that exceeds FCF (real shareholder dilution)
- Management guidance repeatedly below actual results (throwing softballs)

MANAGEMENT COMMENTARY
The earnings call (conference call with analysts) often matters as much as the numbers. Listen for:
- Tone on demand environment: cautious language about "macro uncertainty" can signal weakness
- Guidance: raised, maintained, or cut? Forward EPS guidance is a key stock price driver
- Capital allocation: buybacks (bullish on own stock), dividends, acquisitions (risky)
- Competitive dynamics: pricing power, customer wins/losses

SECTOR-SPECIFIC EARNINGS FOCUS
- Banks: Net Interest Income (NII), Net Interest Margin (NIM), Non-Performing Loans (NPL), Loan Growth
- Retailers: Same-Store Sales, Gross Margin, Inventory Turns
- Energy: Realized oil/gas price, Production volumes, lifting cost per BOE
- Healthcare: Drug trial results, FDA approvals, pipeline updates, payor mix
- Real Estate (REITs): FFO (Funds from Operations), Occupancy Rate, Lease Renewal Spreads
- Tech: ARR, NRR, Cloud Revenue Growth, Seat Count, Backlog

ANALYST RATINGS SYSTEM
- Buy / Overweight / Outperform: Expected to beat the market
- Hold / Neutral / Market Perform: Expected to match the market
- Sell / Underweight / Underperform: Expected to lag the market
Price targets accompany ratings. Consensus price target = average of all analyst targets.
